Alcohol Guide

This provides an approximate guide to your consumption of alcohol in terms of daily units. The Week View shows your total units per week, plus the percentage of calories in your diet that have come from alcohol.

With 7 calories per gram, alcohol contains the second highest number of calories after fat (9 cals/gram), so it is important to keep track of the total number of calories coming from alcoholic drinks, as cutting back may dramatically help your weight loss.

A unit of alcohol is measured by the percentage alcohol by volume (ABV) of a drink multiplied by its volume in litres.

Examples of Units
A pint of strong lager = 6.0 ABV x 568ml / 1000ml = 3.4 units
A 175ml glass of white wine = 12.0 ABV x 175ml / 1000ml = 2.1 units

The maximum guideline amounts for daily and weekly consumption are given below:-

  • No more than 14 units per week, for men and women
  • Spread your drinking throughout the week, but aim for at least two alcohol free days per week
  • Maximum 2-3 units per day (but no more than 14 per week)

The fruit & veg Guide

This provides an approximate guide to your Fruit & Veg consumption.

The tracker is to encourage you to eat more fresh fruit and vegetables. Fruit and veg are a valuable source of vitamins, minerals and fiber. Most are low in calories, making them a great way to bulk out meals.

Each apple icon represents a portion. The apples will turn green when you add a portion of fruit or veg to your food diary. Aim for at least 5 portions a day to get all apple icons lit up green.

What counts as a portion?
  • 80g fresh, frozen or tinned fruit (no portion limit)
  • 80g fresh, frozen or tinned vegetables (no portion limit)
  • 150ml of 100% fruit juice, vegetable juice or smoothies (maximum 1 portion per day)
  • 80g beans and other pulses (maximum 1 portion per day)
  • 30g dried fruit (maximum 1 portion per day)
  • 80g concentrated purees (maximum 1 portion per day)
What doesn't count towards 5-a-day?
  • Fruit and vegetables with any added fats, sugars, or salt
  • Potatoes and other starchy 'staples', such as yams, cassava and plaintains (sweet potatoes DO count)
  • Nuts, seeds, coconut, marmalades and jams
  • Fruit drinks with added ingredients, such as juice 'drinks' and squashes
  • Fruit and herb teas; wines & other alcoholic drinks made from fruit and vegetables

Water Meter Guide

Please note - this is a MANUAL tool. Click on the symbol to add a glass.

Eight 250ml glasses of water (or 2 litres) a day is the target!

The Water Meter is to remind you to drink more fluids. All liquids count towards your fluid intake, with the exception of alcoholic drinks. Ideally try to limit your tea and coffee consumption and drink more pure water, or herbal teas. Cups of tea and coffee do count, but bear in mind they contain caffeine and also have a diuretic effect so are not as hydrating as plain water. (And of course, don't forget to count calories in the milk and any sugar).

If you really don't like water, you can flavour it with fruit squashes. However do check the labels as the 'hi juice' varieties are very high in calories. Also be aware that sugar-free varieties may contain artificial sweeteners such as aspartame.

Think of the benefits! Pure water is great for your skin, aids digestion and can suppress the feeling of hunger - which is sometimes caused by needing fluids.

User Items Guide

We are continually checking the information in our database, it is possible that you may come across a new product before we've had chance to add it.

This feature lets you add a brand new product by entering the data from the packaging.

All foods 'Manually Added' are saved in your personal food database. You can easily find items in future using the Text Search (type in the description) or Barcode Search (enter last 4 digits of the barcode) via the 'Add Food' panel. You can save your manually added items as Favorites and also search and add them to recipes.

Product Title - brand name

If the product is made by a brand or retailer, please enter the brand name e.g. Heinz, Tesco etc If the item is 'generic', that is it doesn't have a brand name, you can leave the box blank e.g. chicken fillets, salmon steaks etc

Product Title - description

Enter a clear description of the item here (excluding the brand name) e.g. Cream of Tomato Soup, or Healthy Eating Balti Cooking Sauce. The clearer the description, the easier it will be to find again in future.

Barcode

Enter the full barcode printed on the packaging (assuming there is one). Only enter the numbers, not the preceding or trailing letters used by some retailers like M&S and Asda.

Serving Size

Enter the serving size you are eating.When entering the data from product packaging, we recommend you use the most meaningful serving size - such as 'Per 1/2 Pack' rather than the figures for 'Per 100g'

This Serving Provides (Cals & Fatg)

Enter the corresponding nutrition data for the serving size you have entered above.

A. No, your manually added foods are saved in your own personal database that others can't see - although the items will appear when you search for them again. You will know they are your manually added foods because they are highlighted in pink.
